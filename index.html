Case Study Mobile App 2024

TasteBuddy

A mobile food-review app that helps users discover the best-tasting takeout meals through real, recent, and meal-specific reviews, reducing the frustration of disappointing food orders. Designed specifically for African cities.

Timeline

8 Weeks

Platform

iOS & Android

Key Responsibilities

🔍 Conduct Research 👤 Define User Needs 🏗️ Information Architecture 🎨 Visual Design 📱 Design Prototypes
⚠️

The Problem

  • ✕ Reviews on existing platforms are scattered and inconsistent
  • ✕ Many reviews are outdated, fake, or overly generic
  • ✕ No platform tells users which specific meals taste good
  • ✕ African users lack tools tailored to local dishes, pricing, and portions
✅

The Solution

  • ✓ Honest, reliable, taste-focused reviews from real customers
  • ✓ Top-rated meals per restaurant with specific recommendations
  • ✓ Fresh reviews with "this week" or "today" filters
  • ✓ Personalized recommendations based on users' taste preferences

Qualitative Analysis

When finding takeout, users typically check familiar food delivery apps, browse nearby restaurants, compare menus and reviews, and decide based on photos, ratings, and past experiences.

Users are disappointed when food doesn't match expectations set by photos, feel frustrated by vague reviews, and wish for clearer details before ordering.

Users check reviews to reduce risk, leave reviews to help others, and value accurate taste descriptions over price or delivery speed.

Empathy Map

🗣️

Say

  • • "It would be helpful if the 5 top-rated meals for each restaurant could be displayed"
  • • "Instead of writing bad reviews online, you can go there in person"
  • • "Reviews should tell me what meal to order, not just how the place looks"
  • • "I'd like an option to upload short videos or pictures"
💭

Think

  • • Wants to see recent reviews to ascertain quality consistency
  • • "I want to save time and avoid bad food experiences"
  • • Does not want continuous scroll to find something decent
  • • Just wants value for money — good food doesn't mean expensive
✍️

Do

  • • Checks delivery speed and distance before choosing
  • • Asks friends or coworkers for "tested and trusted" food spots
  • • Uses Google Maps, TikTok, or WhatsApp to find food spots
  • • Checks Instagram or TikTok for food videos before ordering
❤️

Feel

  • • Frustrated with having to jump between different apps
  • • Does not like when reviews are outdated or fake
  • • Frustrated when meal looks great online but tastes bad
  • • Sick of too many food options with no clear direction
